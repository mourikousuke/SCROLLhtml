(3)Finally, the time the book was closed is recorded in the log when the learner closes the book.

(4)After the closed time is recorded, the log is sent to the database.

4.3Log usage

This system records not only what learners have learned from EPUB, but also some other information

— such as the order of opening and closing pages, learners’ actions (e.g., zooming in or zooming out,

changing of font color or background color, writing in the EPUB, and highlighting of a specific

sentence) in detail.

Logs have two usages: (1) logs are fed back to learners as quizzes (described above), and (2)

e-books are improvable using the logs of learners’ actions (via analyzing the logs). For example, if a

result of the analysis is that many students zoom in on one of the pages, then the page is considered

unreadable.

5.Evaluation and Analysis

We suppose to evaluate this seamless learning system. At first step, we let learners (who take the target

class) to use this system to collect logs. The experimental period is still not decided. As a next step, after

the experimental period, we let learners to answer some questions about this system and observe the

result. In addition, we feed back the result of analysis to learners and teachers, then the teachers improve

their teaching materials, and learners take an examination. We let teachers to answer some question

about the system and observe the result of questions and examination to evaluation. We suppose to

analyzing and feeding back the result with various methods such as social network analysis and

visualization of graph theory (Mouri et al., 2014; 2015).

6.Conclusions and future work

This paper proposes a seamless learning system with EPUB. This system runs on both PCs and mobile

phones. Learners’ record their learning from EPUB in class and their experiences outside of class

through this system. Then, the system allows learners to relate what they have learned in class and what

they have experienced outside of class by providing quizzes or book information.

In future work, this seamless learning system needs to be evaluated regarding its efficacy and

usability through learners’ actual use. In addition, the verification of the effectiveness of this system

will help improve e-books and the system itself is expected to improve through an analysis of the logs

recorded within the system.
